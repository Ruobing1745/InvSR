# Abstract
Diffusion models achieve remarkable performance in image super-resolution (SR), yet they typically adopt an open-loop working mechanism. The low-resolution (LR) image only triggers the generative process once, without verifying whether the generated high-resolution result is reasonable after downsampling reconstruction. To address this issue, this paper explores two lightweight closed-loop correction strategies, both based on the residual formula $r = \text{LR} - D(\text{SR})$, where $D(\cdot)$ represents standard bicubic downsampling. The core difference lies in the correction embedding position. Strategy I is an input-level correction scheme validated on InvSR, which imports the residual into the original LR input to obtain updated input $\text{LR}' = \text{LR} + \lambda\cdot r$, and then performs the secondary diffusion generation process to complete iterative optimization. Strategy II is an output-level correction method applied to BI-DiffSR and S3Diff. It firstly upsamples the residual and directly superimposes it on the initial super-resolution result, namely $\text{SR}' = \text{SR} + \lambda\cdot U(r)$, which brings almost no extra computational overhead.

Both strategies require no additional training and are compatible with various existing diffusion super-resolution frameworks. Experimental results on 1015 test images under ×2 and ×4 super-resolution scales demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods. InvSR achieves an average PSNR gain of 0.56 dB, with 96.7% of test images obtaining performance improvement and the maximum gain reaching 2.37 dB. BI-DiffSR gains an average of 0.27 dB, 92.1% of images are optimized with the highest increment up to 2.90 dB. S3Diff obtains an average improvement of 0.53 dB, and the maximum single-image gain is 4.27 dB. On the unified test subset, Strategy I realizes an average gain of 0.75 dB with a 98.0% improvement ratio, while Strategy II reaches 0.25 dB and 94.0% respectively, showing a performance gap of three times.

In addition, there is almost no correlation between the performance promotion effects of the two strategies on single images with a Pearson correlation coefficient of 0.009, indicating that they can rectify different types of reconstruction errors. Strategy I makes full use of diffusion generative prior information to acquire superior optimization effects, accompanied by doubled computational consumption. In contrast, Strategy II is an efficient low-cost optimization scheme that can bring obvious performance promotion within milliseconds. The optimal adjustment coefficient $\lambda$ varies with different network architectures and is positively correlated with image edge density with a correlation coefficient of approximately 0.4, which facilitates practical engineering deployment. In general, the closed-loop residual correction scheme is a simple, efficient and training-free optimization method, which can be widely applied to various diffusion-based image super-resolution frameworks.
